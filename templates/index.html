What is Battlesnake?

BattleSnake is a programming competition held in Victoria BC. Teams from local schools and tech companies build web-based AIs for the classic arcade game "Snake".

Multiple teams will compete on the same board at the same time, and the winner is the last team slithering!

There will be two tournament brackets. One for Beginners with a grand prize of $300, and a second for Advanced gameplay with a grand prize of $1,000. If you are an experienced BattleSnake veteran then we suggest you enter the Advanced bracket.

This year we are introducing Bounty Snakes, a new way to win prizes throughout the day. Bounty snakes are AIs created by tech companies and sponsors that will be used to test your progress throughout the day. Each Bounty Snake will have a prize attached to it, and the team to defeat each Bounty Snake the fastest will claim its loot.

Lunch, snacks, coffee, and dinner will be provided to throughout the day! Please email battlesnake@sendwithus.com for any food allergies or diet restrictions. Some of the volunteers and developers will be at Felicitas for a meet and greet after the event.

Who

This competition is for students and developers of all skill levels. Participants include: students from University of Victoria, Camosun College, Royal Roads University, Vancouver Island University, as well as local development teams who want to test their mettle in a tournament of an epic proportion.

Why

The purpose is to introduce the fun of developing AIs outside of coding assignments and personal projects. This competition will test your creativity of developing AIs.

Requirements

Have no fear if you are not an expert at coding. This competition is for all skill levels, including novice! Teams consist of 4-5 people, but do not worry if you don’t have a team, lots of individuals sign up! New teams will be formed during registration on February 20th!

Bring your own computer, and please come prepared with Github and Heroku accounts already set up! Please check out our BattleSnake github documents for preparation advice.

We will also be holding a setup tutorial before the competition that is open for all attendees.

Code Review Corner

If you need help getting set up for the event, or have questions about how to get started, there will be a Setup Tutorial at 10:00AM hosted by our main sponsor Heroku. Feel free to come at any time during the day to ask any questions or get help that you feel you may need for the competition. There will also be volunteer developers, you will know them by their orange lanyard, who are always willing to answer questions.
